About  Me

I am a

I'm Courtney Ring and I'm a Marketing Technologist.

So what does this really mean? Let's take a step back to share the bigger picture. We live in a world of all things digital and as a result, marketing and technology are no longer two separate areas of business. With the internet, smartphones, tablets, digital assistants, and wearables - just to name a few - our faces are always in front of screens, and that's exactly where marketing needs to be. Because of this digital revolution, a whole new set of skills are needed to build a bridge between the two areas.

That is where I come in. Some would call me a unicorn, others would refer to me as a hybrid. What it comes down to is this: my passion is all things digital. I want to dive into the development of a website, but also be involved in the initial talks of strategy and design for the site. I want to immerse myself in SEO initiatives to improve content and design to align with best practices, while also monitoring the analytics to measure the success of these initiatives. I want to be the digital ninja who can take a project from start to finish with the expertise to be involved every step of the way. Excited yet? Good. You should be.

For a quick overview of my background, you can download my resume. But I encourage you to scroll down to learn a little more about me and what marketing technology is all about.

Web Development

Web development is the key to bringing ideas and designs to life. And it is not simply about translating words into code, but finding a solution that fits business requirements, follows content and design specs, and most importantly, keeps the code clean and efficient.
With a degree in computer science and a background in IT, I have a lot of experience with various languages and tools for web development. This includes front-end languages and libraries, web frameworks, as well as backend functionality and databases. Additionally, my time with Cigna's Content Management team has given me experience with HP Teamsite Content Manager, as well as custom styling and scripting.

User Interface Design

First impressions are everything when it comes to UI design. Just 10 seconds can determine whether a user will stay or leave. This is why designing a clear and understandable UI is such an integral part of digital strategy.
I have involved myself in a few different design roles. At Cigna, I worked on wireframing and prototyping for both Cigna.com and our customer service application, OneView. For the websites I developed outside of work, I built the UI from scratch, determining everything from colors and fonts to site structure and navigation.

Web Analytics

"If you can't measure it, you can't improve it." That's why I have immersed myself in web analytics. Not only does the data inform the business about who is using the site and what their browsing behavior is, it also provides a solid foundation for making improvements.
As part of my contribution to the Cigna User Experience Team, I provided user data from Google Analytics for the various sites they have launched. These numbers were used to improve both content and user interface practices moving forward. Additionally, I monitor the analytics for the Crawford County Historical Society website and provide reports to the board about its performance.

Digital Content

Writing for the web is like knowing another language. There are many factors involved and I take each one into consideration when crafting content to be read on a screen. Elements like which platform the content is for, what device it will be accessed by, and how it will affect search ranking all can make or break a site's success.
For CCHS, I worked with a board member to write copy for the site that followed SEO best practices. I also worked on content at MPRO, where I wrote online newsletters and copy for their website.
